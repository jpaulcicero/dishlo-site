Questions

Fair questions,
straight answers.

How do I get recipes into Dishlo?

Four ways: snap a photo of a cookbook page, add a screenshot from your camera roll, paste recipe text, or upload a PDF. Dishlo reads it, extracts the ingredients and instructions, and lets you review everything before saving.

Can Dishlo import a recipe from a link?

No — and that's deliberate. Dishlo doesn't scrape websites or fetch content from URLs. Recipes belong to the people who wrote them, so you add the content yourself from sources you have access to. The URL field exists to credit and remember where a recipe came from.

How does the grocery list actually work?

You pick a date range and Dishlo gathers every ingredient from every meal planned in that range, combines duplicates, converts units so amounts add up properly, and sorts everything by store category. Pantry Check lets you hide things you already have before you head out.

Does it work offline?

Yes. Dishlo is offline-first: your recipes, plans, and lists are stored on your device and everything you change saves locally, then syncs automatically when you're back online.

What happens to my data?

It stays yours. No ads, no selling data, and your content is private to your account. Read the plain-English details in our Privacy Policy.

When does Dishlo launch?

Soon — Dishlo is in final testing for Google Play, with the App Store to follow. Email us and we'll let you know the moment it's live.
